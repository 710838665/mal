DEFINITION of
SA1

The full name of SA1 is Statistical Areas Level 1, 2016 Population and Housing Census data is provided as the SA1 level. The Australian Census began in 2011 and has been seven years now.SA1 is usually used as the smallest unit of census data. Australia has 57,523 SA1 areas with no gaps or overlaps. The population of each region ranges from 200 to 800, with an average of 400 people.SA2 (Statistical Region 2 of the Australian Statistical Geographical Standards (ASGS)) is formed by the aggregation of the entire SA1. SA1 is designed not to be connected internally by roads, except for uninhabited island groups, as the islands may not be continuous. Areas such as lakes, commercial districts, and national parks that do not have a resident population are represented by “zero SA1s,” which means that the number of effective designs is equal to zero. Compared to SA2, the main design properties of SA1 are rural or urban. The coding structure of SA1 is an eleven-bit fully layered code or a seven-digit code identifier. The SA1 code for different states may be duplicated because the SA1 code is unique only within the restricted area, preceded by the state and region identifiers. In the future, new code may need to be assigned. If SA1 is revoked, the SA1 identifier will also be eliminated.

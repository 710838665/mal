Data Journalism refers to a new type of news reporting based on data capture, mining, statistics, analysis and visualization. "Two-thirds of Australians drive to work"

According to a census, nearly two-thirds of Australians drive private cars to work, and nearly one-tenth of passengers rely on public transportation. There are 13.3 million registered vehicles in Australia, which is equivalent to one car per 1.37 people. At the same time, Australia's average car travels 12,881 kilometers a year, while all Australian vehicles drive 167 billion kilometers a year.

The ability of the population to access services and employment is strongly influenced by transportation. As you can see from the above pictures, the number of people driving to work as driver exceeds 55% of total employed people. From 2011 to 2016, the change of people driving to work as driver is less than 1% of total employed people. In 2016, men who drove to work as driver were more than 60% of total employed males, while women who chose to drive to work as driver were lower than 55% of total employed females. In terms of the proportion of the population, the proportion of women who choose to drive is lower than that of men. However, the change in women who choose to drive to work as driver from 2011 to 2016 is higher than that of men.

LATTE LINE

The hypothetical boundary (the Latte Line) drawn on the diagonal between Sydney Airport, Parramatta and the northwestern suburbs of Sydney has become the guiding principle for planning the future of the city. It is widely believed that the economic development of the northern part of the Latte Line is better than that of the South.

According to the above picture, the proportion of people who choose to drive to work in the north of the latte line is slightly larger than the south. Because driving is a very important mode of transportation, this is a hard need for many people. So on this issue, the difference between the north and the south of the latte line is very small.

From the map shows that from 2011-2016,the growth rate of people who from Coast Areas choose to drive to work as a driver is higher than the inland areas.This may be because the coastal population has a larger population base and is denser than the inland areas.

In 2011, the total number of men who chose to drive to work was 1,014,681. By 2016, the total number was 1,081,604, an increase of 66,923. The total male growth rate is 6.6%. In 2011, the total number of women who chose to drive to work was 792,667. By 2016, the total number was 871,310, an increase of 78,643. The total female growth rate was 9.9%. In 2011, the total number of people who chose to drive to work was 1,807,348. The total number of people in 2016 was 1,952,914, an increase of 145,566. The total growth rate is 8.1%.
